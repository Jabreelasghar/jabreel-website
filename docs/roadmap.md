# Roadmap

This document records the long-term direction for the website. It is not public site content.

## Current Position

The website presents Dr Jabreel Asghar as a university academic working across AI governance, assessment integrity, educational quality, academic writing, and higher education practice.

The tone should remain serious, academic, restrained, and evidence-informed. The site should not present unsupported claims about consultancy, keynote speaking, awards, grants, media appearances, or institutional influence.

## Near-Term Priorities

- Replace any remaining placeholder content across secondary pages.
- Review and refine the About, Publications, Teaching, Contact, and topic overview pages.
- Move from placeholder topic pages toward accurate content based on CV, publications, teaching, and confirmed professional activity.
- Ensure navigation reflects the actual structure of the site and does not overstate activities.
- Keep the homepage aligned with approved academic positioning.

## Medium-Term Priorities

- Build out the Frameworks & Resources section as real resources become publicly available.
- Add accurate publication records with full citation details.
- Add insight articles only when the article text is actually written or approved.
- Add teaching content that reflects real modules, supervision areas, academic communication teaching, and assessment practice.
- Add talks or presentations only when confirmed or completed.

## Long-Term Direction

The site should become a credible academic knowledge hub focused on:

- Responsible AI in higher education
- Assessment integrity
- Educational quality
- AI-assisted academic writing
- Academic communication
- Curriculum, assessment, and quality enhancement

The website should support academic visibility, research collaboration, professional enquiries, and responsible public communication of research and teaching activity.

## Guardrails

- Do not invent publications, frameworks, workshops, talks, projects, awards, consultancy, grants, or achievements.
- Do not use promotional language such as visionary, thought leader, world-class, transformative, or similar branding claims.
- Do not imply external advisory work, institutional consulting, or keynote activity unless explicitly supported by provided evidence.
- Prefer factual, defensible statements over aspirational positioning.
