# Content Plan

This document records how site content should be organized and expanded. It is not public site content.

## Content Folders

The `content/` directory is organized into:

- `publications/`
- `frameworks/`
- `insights/`
- `teaching/`
- `talks/`

Each folder contains a README explaining what belongs there.

## Publications

Use for real scholarly outputs only:

- Journal articles
- Book chapters
- Conference papers
- Accepted manuscripts
- Other formal academic publications

Do not add publication records until bibliographic details are known.

## Frameworks

Use for practical resources that are ready to describe publicly:

- Responsible AI frameworks
- Assessment integrity guides
- Educational quality resources
- Templates or tools
- Publicly shareable teaching or governance materials

Do not invent frameworks or downloads.

## Insights

Use for website articles and research-informed commentary.

Current approved featured topics:

- Rethinking Assessment in the Age of Generative AI
- Responsible AI Governance in Higher Education
- AI-Assisted Academic Writing: Challenges and Opportunities

These should be treated as featured topics until full article text is written and approved.

## Teaching

Use for accurate teaching-related content:

- English and Academic Communication teaching
- Curriculum and assessment design
- Student learning support
- Supervision interests, if applicable and approved
- Educational practice resources

## Talks

Use only for talks, presentations, seminars, panels, workshops, or invited sessions that are confirmed or completed.

Do not add speculative speaking content.

## Content Review Rules

Before publishing new content, check:

- Is the claim factual?
- Is the status clear?
- Is the date accurate?
- Does it avoid promotional exaggeration?
- Does it avoid implying services or achievements not provided by the user?
- Does it fit the site's academic tone?
