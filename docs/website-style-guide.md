# Website Style Guide

This document records design and writing rules for future website work. It is not public site content.

## Design Principles

- Preserve a restrained academic visual style.
- Keep layouts clear, readable, and calm.
- Avoid corporate, startup, or sales-oriented visual language.
- Do not redesign existing sections unless a change is necessary for readability, accessibility, or content accuracy.
- Maintain consistent spacing, typography, and responsive behavior.

## Typography and Layout

- Use existing typography, section spacing, card styling, and layout patterns unless there is a clear reason to change them.
- Keep headings direct and descriptive.
- Avoid oversized marketing-style hero copy outside the homepage.
- Avoid nested cards or overly decorative panels.

## Homepage Rules

The homepage should communicate:

- Academic identity
- Research areas
- Professional practice areas
- Topic overview pages
- Research insights
- Contact for academic networking or collaboration

The homepage should not imply:

- Commercial consulting
- Unverified speaking activity
- A completed library of downloadable resources
- Published articles that are only planned topics

## Content Cards

Cards may represent:

- Topic overview pages
- Featured topics
- Actual publications
- Actual resources

Cards should not represent fabricated downloads, frameworks, dates, reading times, or publication status.

## Buttons

Use restrained button labels:

- Explore My Research
- Resources & Frameworks
- Learn More
- View topic
- Contact Me

Avoid:

- Get started
- Book now
- Hire me
- Transform your practice

## Accessibility and Readability

- Keep text concise.
- Use descriptive links.
- Ensure buttons and cards work on mobile and desktop.
- Avoid dense paragraphs on overview pages.
- Ensure all homepage links resolve to real pages.
